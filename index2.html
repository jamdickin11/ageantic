5 major trends set to shape travel in 2026

Published Thu, Dec 25 2025 1:10 AM EST
Updated Thu, Dec 25 2025 1:36 AM EST
Authors: Kaela Ling, Monica Pitrelli

Traveling is no longer just about the destination ‚Äì or even the journey. It's about the experience you walk away with. Since the end of Covid, experience‚Äëled travel has continued to evolve, shifting away from checking off landmarks and more towards meaningful trips that help travelers connect with the people and places they visit.

1. The anti-tourist

To better experience local culture, avoid contributing to overtourism, and potentially save money, travelers are increasingly avoiding peak seasons and heavily visited destinations. This shift is fueling the rise of "secondary cities" across ASEAN like Chiang Rai (Thailand), Malacca (Malaysia), and Siem Reap (Cambodia).

2. 'Decision detox' holidays

Today's travelers are mentally exhausted and increasingly outsourcing planning to AI agents that offer personalized, all-inclusive packages. As cognitive overload becomes a defining feature of modern life, decision-light travel is becoming a core expectation.

3. Wellness moves beyond spas

Wellness travel has evolved from spa packages to science-backed longevity programs and mental health-focused trips. In ASEAN, this includes meditation retreats in Thailand, traditional healing in Bali, and jungle wellness experiences in Malaysia.

4. Nostalgic travel

Younger travelers are seeking vacations that take them down memory lane, while new retirees are embracing "golden gap year" trips across Southeast Asia, taking extended journeys that are more adventurous than retiree trips of the past.

5. AI in hotel service

Hotels are using AI to predict guest wants before they arrive, personalizing stays with unprecedented precision. In ASEAN, AI agents can customize everything from room amenities to cultural experiences based on individual preferences.
